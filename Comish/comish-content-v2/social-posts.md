### Post 2: The Math
```
Quick math for my sales friends:

You're at $340K for the quarter.
Your quota is $375K.
You're at 91% attainment.

Your comp plan:
- Tier 1 (0-100%): 6%
- Tier 2 (100-125%): 8%  
- Tier 3 (125-150%): 10%
- Tier 4 (150%+): 12%

A $50K deal just came in.

What's your commission on that deal?

Most reps would say: "$3,000" (6% of $50K)

Wrong.

Here's the real math:
- $35K fills you to quota (6%) = $2,100
- $15K is at Tier 2 rates (8%) = $1,200
- Total: $3,300

That's $300 more than you thought.

Now multiply that across a full year of deals.

This is why tracking matters.

#sales #commission #money #salesrep
```

### Post 3: Red Flags
```
5 comp plan red flags every sales rep should know:

1. "Effective commission rate" instead of actual rates
They're hiding the math. Ask for the real numbers.

2. Retroactive quota changes mid-quarter
If they can move the goalposts, your accelerators mean nothing.

3. Commission paid on "collected revenue" not closed revenue
You closed it in March. Customer pays in June. You might be gone by then.

4. Clawbacks beyond 90 days
Churn happens. But 6-month clawbacks are punishment, not policy.

5. "Up to" language everywhere
"Earn up to 15% commission" means almost nobody earns 15%.

Your comp plan is a contract. Read it like one.
```
